7. Wider Issues, Risks & Mitigation

For each risk: identify the risk → explain the impact → explain the mitigation.
Focus on realistic, technical mitigations — not vague statements like “use security”.

Risk: unauthorised account access
Impact: data breach, reputational damage, financial loss
Mitigation: hashed passwords, HTTPS, secure authentication, rate limiting


Risk: personal data misuse / GDPR breach
Impact: legal penalties, loss of trust
Mitigation: data minimisation, user consent, secure database storage


Risk: incorrect calculations / incorrect system output
Impact: unsafe decisions, financial loss, user harm
Mitigation: validated inputs, tested formulas, verification checks


Risk: system not usable by disabled users
Impact: exclusion, legal non-compliance (Equality Act)
Mitigation: WCAG guidelines, high contrast UI, keyboard navigation, screen reader compatibility


GDPR, Computer Misuse Act, Equality Act, industry-specific regulation

visual / interface design

indicative content and marker guidance

Minimize the user's memory load by making objects, actions, and options visible. The user should not have to remember information from one part to another should be clearly shown in the design The design should always show clients what the users are informed about, what is going on, through appropriate feedback messages or snippets The design should not contain information which is irrelevant or rarely needed. Every piece of extra information competes with the relevant information and diminishes their relative visibility. So, the use of images which have little or no relevance to the product or brand should be avoided. The design should also consider the following factors:

  • Consistency and standards
  • Aesthetic and minimalist design
  • Help and documentation
  • Consistent fonts and colours and imagery which help users know that all pieces of the solution are working together and are pieces of a consistent whole.
  • Use of space
  • Space between lines, called leading, is important for readability.
  • Ensure the space is not too small to avoid making it harder for readers to follow the line properly.
  • However, too much space, readers can wander away from the text altogether, check for lots of white space; however, depending on the design does not necessarily mean it wasted space.
  • Look for imagery used in the websites or app as much as possible because this further helps users skim the content and digest material quickly and easily.
